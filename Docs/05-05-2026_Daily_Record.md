Today we revisited our project vision and explored three different ideas related to a document agent system. After discussion and evaluation, we finalized a concept inspired by a BookStack-like platform tailored for MDH University. The goal is to create a centralized system where staff can upload and manage academic or administrative information, and students can easily access updates without needing to directly contact professors or administrative staff. This approach aims to improve communication, reduce repetitive queries, and provide a single source of truth for university-related information.

We then focused on understanding user needs by creating two key personas: a professor persona and a student persona. Based on these personas, we developed practical scenarios to reflect real-world usage. In the professor scenario, the user uploads course materials, announcements, and updates to the platform for easy access. In the student scenario, the user regularly checks the platform to stay informed about updates, deadlines, and resources, eliminating the need for frequent emails or in-person queries.

Finally, we discussed our project idea with other groups in the class and reviewed their visions and scenarios as well. We presented our concept by explaining the personas, scenarios, and overall vision, which helped us gather feedback and refine our approach. This collaborative discussion allowed us to validate our idea, improve clarity, and ensure that our solution effectively addresses real user problems within the university environment.
